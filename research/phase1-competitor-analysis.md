## Executive Summary

The Indian homework-solving AI app market in 2025-2026 is characterized by massive disruption. Several once-dominant players have collapsed (BYJU'S, Toppr, Socratic), while global AI-native apps (Gauth, Question AI) are rapidly capturing market share. The key findings are:

- **Market Vacuum:** BYJU'S collapse (insolvency, app delisted), Socratic shutdown (merged into Google Lens), and Toppr's uncertain fate have left millions of Indian students without their go-to apps.
- **Price Sensitivity:** Indian students overwhelmingly demand free or sub-$5/month solutions. Apps priced above $10/month see minimal Indian adoption.
- **Language Gap:** No major homework AI app offers deep vernacular Indian language support beyond basic Hindi. This is a massive untapped opportunity across 22 official languages.
- **Board Alignment Gap:** Global apps (Photomath, Gauth, Question AI) lack CBSE/ICSE/State Board curriculum alignment. Indian apps that had it (BYJU'S, Toppr) are dead or dying.
- **AI Shift:** The market is moving from video-solution libraries to real-time AI-generated solutions. Doubtnut's video-first model is increasingly outdated.

## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### 1. Doubtnut (Allen Group)

**Status:** Active but experiencing reliability issues. Some users report the app not opening (late 2025/early 2026). The Softonic download was marked "no longer available" at one point. Now part of the Allen Group (India's largest coaching institute).

**Strengths:**
- Massive video solution library specifically for Indian curriculum
- Hindi and English medium support
- Deep NCERT, JEE, NEET content alignment
- Completely free for doubt solving
- Photo-to-video-solution matching (unique approach)

**Weaknesses:**
- Video-first model is slower than real-time AI answers
- Reported wrong solutions and untrustable answers (Quora complaints)
- Payment/access issues -- users charged but denied access
- Poor customer service; slow technical team
- App stability issues in late 2025

**India Relevance:** HIGH -- purpose-built for Indian students, NCERT-aligned, free.

---

### 2. Photomath (Google)

**Status:** Active and stable. Google acquired it in March 2023. Over 100M downloads. Being integrated with Google Lens ecosystem.

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class math OCR and camera scanning
- Excellent step-by-step breakdown with multiple methods
- Handwriting recognition
- Clean, ad-free free tier for basic math
- 32-language localization

**Weaknesses:**
- **Math only** -- no science, humanities, or other subjects
- Premium features ($69.99/yr) are expensive for Indian students
- No NCERT/CBSE/ICSE curriculum alignment
- No Hindi-medium textbook solutions
- No live tutor or community features
- Requires internet connection

**India Relevance:** MEDIUM -- excellent for math but too narrow and not India-localized.

---

### 3. Gauth (ByteDance)

**Status:** Active and aggressive growth. Backed by ByteDance. Top-2 education app on Google Play.

**Strengths:**
- Multi-subject (30+ subjects including STEM, economics, coding)
- AI chat follow-ups ("why did you factor this way?")
- Live tutor escalation for hard problems
- Homework planner with progress tracking
- 100M+ question bank
- Ad-free across all tiers
- ~95% solve rate

**Weaknesses:**
- Complex calculus/proofs have lower accuracy
- Humanities responses are generic
- Messy handwriting causes failures
- Aggressive monetization -- verified complaints of unexpected charges (users charged $44-50 after "free trials")
- No India-specific board alignment (CBSE/ICSE)
- No Hindi-medium content
- Free tier is heavily limited

**India Relevance:** MEDIUM -- strong product but too expensive and not India-localized.

---

### 4. Socratic by Google

**Status:** SHUT DOWN. Removed from app stores. Features folded into Google Lens.

**Legacy Features Worth Noting:**
- Simple photo-to-answer interface (set the standard for snap-and-solve)
- Free, no ads, no subscriptions
- Multi-subject (not just math)
- Google AI-powered explanations

**Why It Died:** Google decided to consolidate its AI efforts. Socratic's core functionality is now inside Google Lens, but Lens is a general-purpose tool, not a dedicated homework helper. This left a gap for dedicated homework apps.

**India Relevance:** The shutdown created a massive user vacuum in India where Socratic was popular among English-medium students.

---

### 5. Question AI (Zuoyebang)

**Status:** Active and rapidly growing. Top-ranked education app globally per Sensor Tower (mid-2024). Nearly 2M weekly active users in North America alone. 10M+ downloads.

**Strengths:**
- Excellent snap & solve with high accuracy (95%+)
- Multi-subject coverage
- AI chat for follow-up questions
- PDF homework upload (unique -- great for worksheet assignments)
- Text summarizer tool
- 1M+ step-by-step video library
- 100+ language support
- Affordable annual plan ($59/yr)
- Chrome extension for desktop homework

**Weaknesses:**
- Free tier has daily limits and ads
- Ads persist even in some premium tiers
- Complex multi-step calculus explanations can be rushed
- No India-specific board alignment
- Chinese parent company (Zuoyebang) may face regulatory scrutiny in India
- No live tutor option

**India Relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH -- great value, multi-language, but no India board alignment and potential regulatory risk (Chinese-linked ownership through Zuoyebang).

---

### 6. BYJU'S

**Status:** EFFECTIVELY NON-OPERATIONAL. Android app delisted from Play Store (May 2025) due to unpaid AWS bills. Insolvency proceedings ongoing. Founder faces $1B+ personal liability in US courts. Once valued at $22B, now worth $0.

**What Was Good (for historical reference):**
- Adaptive learning paths personalized to each student
- Comprehensive K-12 coverage for CBSE, ICSE, State Boards
- Disney-quality animated video lessons
- Offline access to downloaded content
- Massive brand recognition in India

**What Went Wrong:**
- Predatory sales tactics (late-night calls, pressure on parents)
- Exorbitant pricing (₹26,000 to ₹1,60,000/yr)
- Burned through $5B+ in funding
- Accounting fraud allegations
- $533M transferred to a Miami hedge fund (called "straight up fraud" by US judge)

**India Relevance:** The collapse of BYJU'S has left tens of millions of Indian students who relied on it without an alternative. This represents the single largest market opportunity.

---

### 7. Vedantu

**Status:** Active but scaling back. Still operational with live classes, AI mentor, and doubt solving.

**Strengths:**
- Live interactive classes with real-time Q&A
- AI mentor "Ved" for 24/7 doubt solving
- Strong JEE/NEET coaching (Eklavya Batch 2026)
- CBSE, ICSE, State Board coverage
- In-class quizzes with leaderboards
- Hindi and English medium
- 14-day free trial

**Weaknesses:**
- Extremely poor customer service (2.6/5 on PissedConsumer, 1.27/5 on MouthShut)
- Aggressive, unethical sales tactics (late-night calls, misrepresenting agreements)
- Refund issues -- users report being denied refunds and unable to reach management
- Misleading course information (e.g., CBSE content sold as Maharashtra Board)
- Expensive JEE/NEET courses (₹92,544 - ₹1,14,066 for 2 years)
- Material shipping delays
- Not a pure homework-solving app (more of a coaching platform)

**India Relevance:** HIGH for coaching, but too expensive and poorly managed for mass-market homework solving.

---

### 8. Toppr

**Status:** UNCERTAIN. Acquired by BYJU'S in 2021 for $150M. Fate tied to BYJU'S insolvency proceedings. Likely non-operational.

**What Was Good:**
- Adaptive practice engine
- CBSE, ICSE, State Board alignment
- JEE/NEET mock tests
- Video lectures in Hindi and English
- IIT Bombay founders brought strong technical credibility

**India Relevance:** The loss of Toppr alongside BYJU'S compounds the market vacuum for board-aligned homework tools.

---

### 9. Brainly

**Status:** Active. 350M+ global users. India-specific version (brainly.in) available.

**Strengths:**
- Massive community of students and experts answering questions
- AI tutor with step-by-step explanations
- Scan & solve functionality
- Personalized quizzes, study guides, flashcards
- NCERT, CBSE, ICSE, State Board content on Indian version
- JEE, NEET, AIIMS, NTSE, UPSC coverage
- Multi-subject (20+)
- Affordable ($39/yr for Plus)

**Weaknesses:**
- Answer quality varies wildly (community-sourced, so spam and incorrect answers exist)
- No Hindi or vernacular language support for the app interface
- Unexpected charges and difficult refund process reported
- Customer service issues
- Not AI-first -- still heavily reliant on community answers which can be slow
- Ad-heavy free tier

**India Relevance:** HIGH -- one of the few active platforms with India-specific board content, but quality inconsistency is a major problem.

---

### 10. Chegg

**Status:** Active but in severe decline. Stock down 97% from peak. 636 employees laid off in 2025. Revenue falling 30% YoY. Being crushed by ChatGPT.

**Strengths:**
- Massive textbook solutions database (120M+ archived expert answers)
- New multi-LLM comparison feature (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek side by side)
- Writing tools with plagiarism checking
- Chegg India expert network provides earning opportunity for Indian graduates

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive for Indian students ($15.95-$19.95/mo = ~₹1,330-₹1,660/mo)
- Primarily US-focused content and textbooks
- No NCERT/CBSE/ICSE alignment
- AI responses replacing human expert quality
- Weekly limits even on paid plans
- Not competitive against free ChatGPT for most use cases

**India Relevance:** LOW for Indian students as users; HIGH for India as an expert-supply market (Chegg India hires answer-writers).

## Dream Feature Matrix

### Tier 1: Core Homework Solving (Must-Have)

| Feature | Inspiration Source | Implementation |

| **Instant Photo Scan & Solve** | Photomath, Gauth, Question AI | Camera scan of printed/handwritten questions with <3 second AI response |

## Market Gaps and Opportunities

### Gap 1: The Post-BYJU'S Vacuum (MASSIVE)
- 50M+ BYJU'S users are now without an app
- Toppr (10M+ users) is also effectively dead
- Socratic (popular among English-medium students) has shut down
- **Combined displaced users: 60-70M students**
- No single app has captured these users yet

### Gap 2: Vernacular Language AI Homework Help (UNTAPPED)
- 22 official Indian languages; apps support English and basic Hindi at best
- 70%+ of students study in vernacular medium
- Speech recognition for Indian accents/dialects is underdeveloped
- **First app to offer quality homework help in Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Marathi wins a massive market**

### Gap 3: State Board Alignment (IGNORED)
- Over 30 State Education Boards in India
- Only Toppr (dead) and Vedantu (expensive) had meaningful State Board support
- Global apps have zero State Board content
- UP Board alone has 27M+ students; Maharashtra SSC has 15M+ students
- **State Board content is the single largest underserved segment**

### Gap 4: Affordable Pricing (BROKEN)
- BYJU'S charged ₹26K-₹1.6L/yr
- Vedantu charges ₹92K+ for JEE courses
- Chegg charges ₹1,330/mo
- Indian student households spend ₹500-2,000/mo on tutoring
- **An app at ₹99-199/mo with free core features would dominate**

### Gap 5: Trust Deficit (OPPORTUNITY)
- BYJU'S destroyed trust with predatory sales, fraud, and collapse
- Vedantu has terrible customer service reputation
- Gauth has billing scam complaints
- **A transparent, no-sales-calls, easy-refund app would win on trust alone**

### Gap 6: Offline-First Design (NEGLECTED)
- All AI homework apps require stable internet
- 40%+ of Indian students have unreliable connectivity
- Rural India (65% of population) is severely underserved
- **Pre-cached NCERT solutions + offline queue for doubts would be revolutionary**
